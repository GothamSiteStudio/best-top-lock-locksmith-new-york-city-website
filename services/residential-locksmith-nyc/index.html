Residential Service

Security work built for how New Yorkers actually live

Residential locksmith work in New York City is rarely generic. Apartment doors, management rules, co-op approvals, old cylinders, and building-specific entry setups all affect the right solution. Some customers only need a rekey after a roommate or dog walker moves out. Others need a full lock change after moving into a new unit, upgrading a weak bottom lock, or replacing hardware that has been sticking for months.

We built this page around those real scenarios. Our residential service focuses on entry doors first, because that is where the biggest practical security gains usually happen. If your current setup is weak, we can explain whether a simple rekey is enough or whether a stronger top lock, deadbolt, or high-security cylinder is the smarter move. If the door already has quality hardware, we can keep the work efficient and avoid recommending unnecessary parts.

When residential locksmith service makes sense

  • You just moved in and want control over who still has keys to the apartment.
  • Your lock is sticking, loose, misaligned, or showing wear before it fails completely.
  • You want stronger apartment security with a top lock or deadbolt upgrade.
  • You are comparing a smart lock against a traditional keyed setup and need guidance.
